Tech Looks Pretty. It Isn’t.
April 3, 2026 • Tech

From the outside, tech looks… clean. Minimal UI, smooth animations, everything just works. But the deeper you go, the more you realise — none of this is actually simple.

Every button you click has layers behind it. Logic, APIs, edge cases, bugs waiting to happen. And somehow, all of that chaos gets packaged into something that looks effortless.

That contrast is what surprised me the most. The prettier something looks on the screen, the messier it usually is underneath.

And when you're new to this space, it can feel overwhelming. There’s always something you don’t know. Some tool you’ve never used. Some concept everyone else seems to “just get.”

But honestly? That confusion never fully goes away. You just get better at navigating it.

I’ve stopped trying to “master everything” and started focusing on just moving forward, one thing at a time. Break things, fix them, repeat.

One thing that helped me a lot — learning Git and GitHub. Not in a “memorise commands” way, but actually using it while building things.

It teaches you how real development works: version control, mistakes, collaboration, fixing your own chaos.

If you’re just starting out or feeling lost, you’re not behind — you’re just in the middle of the process.

Tech isn’t clean. It isn’t straightforward. But that’s kind of what makes it interesting.

I’m still figuring things out too. Just a little less confused than yesterday.
